Why learn variable, let, const?


These keywords are used to declare variables in JavaScript.
Understanding their differences is crucial for writing predictable and maintainable code.
var:
1. Function-scoped: The scope of a var variable is limited to the function where it is declared.
2. Can be re-declared and updated.
3. Does not enforce block-level scope, which can lead to unexpected results.
let:
1. Block-scoped: The scope is limited to the block, statement, or expression where it is used.
2. Cannot be re-declared in the same scope but can be updated.
const:
1. Block-scoped, like let.
2. Cannot be re-declared or updated once assigned.
3. Useful for declaring constants or variables that should not change.
